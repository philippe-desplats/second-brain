# Client Onboarding Playbook

_Distilled from the Globex onboarding on 2026-06-22. See Relations for sources._

A repeatable five-step sequence to take a new client from signed to productive without losing the first two weeks to back-and-forth. Each step ends with a concrete artifact so nothing stays implicit.

## Step 1, Confirm scope and a single point of contact

- Restate the scope in one paragraph and get it acknowledged in writing.
- Name one decision-maker on the client side, everything routes through them.
- Capture any hard constraints early (blackout days, review windows, tooling limits).

## Step 2, Collect assets and access

- Send one checklist covering content exports, analytics, and any credentials.
- Prefer read-only exports to start, request full access only when scope is locked.
- Note the owner of each asset so follow-ups go to the right person.

## Step 3, Run a tight kickoff

- Share a short agenda the day before, three items maximum.
- Leave the call with locked decisions, owned actions, and dated deadlines.
- Invite the person who owns the underlying data, not just the sponsor.

## Step 4, Set up the shared workspace and cadence

- Create the project folder and drop the brief, emails, and minutes in it.
- Agree on a fixed weekly sync slot that respects the client's constraints.
- Write the first deliverable date into the brief so the client can quote it internally.

## Step 5, Send the recap and baseline plan

- Within a day of kickoff, send minutes with decisions and action owners.
- Confirm the next deliverable and its date so momentum carries.
- Log risks and open questions where they will be seen at the next sync.
